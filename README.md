# GASP-CD

We study quickest changepoint detection across $n$ graph-structured data streams when only $B < n$ can be observed per round. We characterise the minimax detection delay in terms of an \emph{information coverage rate} $\mathcal{R}(G,B)$ that captures the interaction between graph topology and observation budget, and a spectral quantity $\lambda_2/\lambda_n$ governing indirect information diffusion. Specifically, we prove that the minimax delay satisfies $d^* = \Theta(\mathcal{R_{\textrm{eff}}}(G,B,s) \cdot \sigma^{2} \log (n/\alpha)/\delta_{\mu^{2}})$, where $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{eff}}$ unifies both mechanisms. We instantiate this for complete graphs, cycles, expanders, and stars. Our algorithm, GASP-CD, combines a variance-adaptive windowed Bernstein GLR statistic with a graph-aware sampling index, achieving near-minimax optimality: its delay is within a universal constant factor of the lower bound. GASP-CD adapts to unknown variances, yielding up to $\textrm{R}^2/\sigma^2$ improvement over Hoeffding-based methods, and degrades gracefully when graph structure is absent or misspecified. Experiments on synthetic graph families and the CICIDS2017 intrusion dataset confirm the theoretical scaling predictions.
